What is Dairy Farming?

Dairy farming is a specialized and organized branch of agriculture that focuses on the long-term production of milk from domesticated animals such as cows, buffaloes, goats, and sheep. Unlike traditional animal rearing, dairy farming involves planned breeding, scientific feeding, proper housing, and regular healthcare to ensure consistent and high-quality milk production.

Milk produced in dairy farms is either consumed directly as fresh milk or sent to dairy processing units where it is converted into value-added products such as butter, ghee, curd, paneer, cheese, yogurt, and milk powder. This makes dairy farming not only an agricultural activity but also an important part of the food industry and rural economy.

In countries like India, dairy farming plays a crucial role in livelihood generation, employment creation, and food security. Millions of small and marginal farmers depend on dairy farming as a steady source of income.

Core Activities in Dairy Farming

Cattle Housing

Clean, ventilated, and comfortable sheds that protect animals from extreme weather and reduce stress.

Feeding & Nutrition

Balanced diet including green fodder, dry fodder, and concentrates to improve milk yield.

Veterinary Care

Regular vaccination, deworming, and health check-ups to prevent diseases.

Milking & Storage

Hygienic milking, stainless steel containers, and immediate cooling to maintain milk quality.
