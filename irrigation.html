Irrigation

Efficient water management techniques for sustainable agriculture.

üíß What is Irrigation?

Irrigation is the artificial supply of water to crops through canals, wells, tube wells, sprinklers, or drip systems. It is used in areas where rainfall is insufficient, irregular, or seasonal.

Proper irrigation improves crop yield, reduces drought risks, and supports sustainable farming.

üåä Sources of Irrigation

Surface Water Sources

Surface water includes rivers, canals, lakes, and reservoirs. Rainwater is stored in large dams and distributed through canals to farmlands.

This method is very effective for large agricultural regions but depends heavily on rainfall and river flow.

  • Examples: Rivers, Canals, Lakes, Dams
  • Best suited for large farming areas
  • Requires good canal maintenance
Groundwater Sources

Groundwater is stored underground in porous rocks and soil layers. Farmers extract this water using wells, tube wells, and bore wells.

It is reliable but excessive use can lower the water table, so sustainable usage is important.

  • Examples: Wells, Tube wells, Bore wells
  • Most commonly used in rural India
  • Needs careful management
Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting involves collecting and storing rainwater in tanks, ponds, or underground reservoirs for later use in irrigation.

It reduces dependence on groundwater and is very useful in drought-prone areas.

  • Eco-friendly and cost-effective
  • Helps recharge groundwater
  • Useful in water-scarce regions
