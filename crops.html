üå± What is a Crop?

Crops are plants grown on a large scale for food, fiber, fodder, and industrial use. Farmers prepare land, sow seeds, irrigate, and manage fields in a planned way ‚Äî this process is called cultivation.

Crops support agriculture and the economy by providing food, raw materials, and employment.

Kharif (June‚ÄìOctober): Grown in the rainy season and need more water. Examples: Rice, Maize, Cotton.

Rabi (October‚ÄìMarch): Grown in winter, need cooler climate and less water. Examples: Wheat, Mustard, Barley.

Wheat
What is Wheat?

Wheat is one of the most important cereal grains in the world, belonging to the genus Triticum.

Season (Rabi)

Sown: October‚ÄìNovember | Harvested: March‚ÄìApril

Cultivation

Wheat requires well-drained loamy soil and a cool climate. Land is ploughed and leveled before sowing. Irrigation is provided at key growth stages such as tillering and grain formation.

Importance
  • Major staple food crop worldwide
  • Supports bakery, pasta, and milling industries
  • Provides employment in rural areas
Harvesting & Storage

Wheat is harvested using combine harvesters or manually in some regions. It is stored in silos or warehouses.

Rice
What is Rice?

Rice is a staple cereal crop and the primary food source for more than half of the world's population.

Season (Kharif)

Sown: June‚ÄìJuly | Harvested: October‚ÄìNovember

Cultivation

Rice requires high water availability. Fields are puddled and transplanted with seedlings.

Importance
  • Staple food in Asia and Africa
  • Supports rice milling and export industries
  • Provides livelihood to millions of farmers
Harvesting & Storage

Rice is harvested using machines or manually and stored in granaries or mills.

Maize (Corn)
What is Maize?

Maize is a widely cultivated cereal used as food, animal feed, and industrial raw material.

Season

Kharif: June‚ÄìJuly | Rabi: October‚ÄìNovember

Cultivation

Maize grows best in warm climates with moderate rainfall. Fields are ploughed and seeds are sown in rows.

Importance
  • Major animal feed source
  • Used in biofuel and starch industries
  • Consumed as sweet corn and cornflakes
Harvesting & Storage

Harvested when kernels are mature and stored in dry conditions.

Vegetables
What are Vegetables?

Vegetables refer to edible plant parts such as roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, or seeds that are consumed mainly as savory food.

Classification (Based on Plant Parts)
  • Root: Potato, carrot, beetroot, radish
  • Leafy: Spinach, lettuce, cabbage
  • Fruit: Tomato, brinjal, capsicum
  • Bulb: Onion, garlic
  • Flower: Broccoli, cauliflower
Season (India)
  • Kharif: Tomato, Brinjal, Ladyfinger
  • Rabi: Potato, Cabbage, Cauliflower
  • Year-round: Onion, Garlic
Cultivation

Vegetables are grown in open fields, greenhouses, or through hydroponics. Some are directly sown, while others are transplanted.

Harvesting & Storage

Harvested manually or by machines; stored in cold storage or processed.

Fruits
What are Fruits?

Fruits are the edible reproductive parts of flowering plants that usually contain seeds.

Season (India)
  • Perennial: Banana, Papaya, Guava
  • Kharif: Watermelon, Muskmelon
  • Rabi: Orange, Grapes
  • Summer: Mango, Litchi
Cultivation

Fruit crops are usually grown from saplings. Drip irrigation is commonly used.

Harvesting & Storage

Fruits are harvested at maturity and stored in cold storage.
