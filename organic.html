What is Organic Farming?

Organic farming is not just a method ‚Äî it is a philosophy. It believes in working with nature, not against it. Instead of chemicals, it uses compost, manure, and natural processes to grow food that is safe, nutritious, and sustainable.

From healthy soil to clean water and biodiversity, organic farming builds a balanced ecosystem for future generations.

Key Features
  • Use of natural manure and compost
  • No chemical pesticides
  • Emphasis on soil fertility
  • Eco-friendly farming
Benefits
  • Healthy food
  • Better soil quality
  • Cleaner water
  • Environmental protection

Methods of Organic Farming

These scientific and traditional methods help maintain soil fertility, protect biodiversity, and ensure sustainable farming.

Composting

Composting is the process of decomposing organic waste such as crop residue, kitchen waste, and animal manure into rich organic fertilizer.

  • Improves soil structure
  • Increases nutrient availability
  • Reduces chemical fertilizer use
Crop Rotation

Crop rotation involves growing different crops in the same field in different seasons.

  • Prevents soil nutrient depletion
  • Reduces pest and disease attacks
  • Maintains soil fertility
Mulching

Mulching is covering the soil with organic materials like straw, leaves, or compost.

  • Retains soil moisture
  • Prevents weed growth
  • Protects soil from erosion
Natural Pest Control

This method uses beneficial insects and natural predators instead of chemical pesticides.

  • Protects crops naturally
  • Maintains ecological balance
  • Safe for humans and animals

Types of Organic Fertilizers

These natural fertilizers improve soil fertility without harming the environment.

Compost

Decomposed organic waste that enriches soil with nutrients.

Vermicompost

Produced by earthworms; improves soil structure and fertility.

Cow Dung Manure

Traditional natural fertilizer rich in organic matter.

Green Manure

Grown crops plowed back into soil to increase nutrients.

Biofertilizers

Beneficial microbes that enhance nutrient availability.

Liquid Organic Fertilizer

Made from compost tea or fermented plant extracts.
