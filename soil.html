Understanding soil quality for sustainable and productive agriculture.

Soil health refers to the ability of soil to function as a living ecosystem that sustains plants, animals, and humans. Healthy soil supports plant growth, improves water retention, and enhances biodiversity.

Good soil health is essential for sustainable agriculture because it ensures nutrient availability, prevents soil erosion, and reduces dependency on chemical fertilizers.

What is Soil Testing?

Soil testing is a scientific method in which soil samples are collected from fields and analyzed in a laboratory. It helps farmers understand nutrient levels, pH value, and fertility of soil.

Why is Soil Testing Important?
  • Correct use of fertilizers
  • Prevents overuse of chemicals
  • Improves crop yield
  • Reduces farming cost
  • Maintains long-term soil health
How is Soil Testing Done?

Farmers collect soil from different parts of the field using a soil auger or spade.

The collected samples are mixed, dried, and prepared for testing.

The sample is tested in a lab for pH, nutrients (NPK), and organic matter.
What Does a Soil Test Report Tell?

Shows whether soil is acidic, neutral, or alkaline.

Shows levels of Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium.

Indicates organic content which improves fertility.
